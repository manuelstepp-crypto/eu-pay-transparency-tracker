## Script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:08]**
Forty days to the EU Pay Transparency deadline — and only one Member State has actually crossed the finish line.

**[CONTEXT — 0:08–0:18]**
This is the week 18 update on Directive 2023/970. As of mid-April, no country has fully completed transposition, and the European Commission has confirmed that 7 June 2026 is firm — no postponements.

**[HIGHLIGHT 1 — 0:18–0:35]**
Slovakia is officially first past the post. Lewis Silkin's deep-dive this week confirms the Equal Pay Act adopted on 15 April enters into force on the EU deadline itself. Compliant pay structures are required by 31 July — barely seven weeks later. And same-sex equal pay claims are in scope.

**[HIGHLIGHT 2 — 0:35–0:50]**
Spain's draft Royal Decree consultation went live on 24 April — closes 8 May. Ireland's government has now openly confirmed it will miss the deadline, and a Mason Hayes & Curran survey of 500 employers found one in five view the rules as an "unnecessary burden". And in Belgium, Minister Clarinval has reaffirmed the federal ambition to fully transpose by 7 June — no draft yet.

**[HIGHLIGHT 3 — 0:50–1:05]**
Czechia closed its consultation on 27 April and is openly opting for a "minimalist" transposition — substantive obligations not until 2028. Estonia's headline-grabbing refusal has softened: salary-history bans and pay-range disclosure stay in.

**[CTA — 1:05–1:20]**
Country-by-country status, key dates and sources are live on the tracker. Link in the description. Subscribe for next week's update.
