## Post

**40 days to the EU Pay Transparency deadline. Here's where week 18 leaves us.**

🟢 **Slovakia** stays the only Member State across the line. Lewis Silkin's deep-dive this week confirmed the Equal Pay Act (adopted 15 April) enters into force on 7 June — and demands compliant pay structures by 31 July. Same-sex equal pay claims are in scope.

🟡 **Spain** opened public consultation on the draft Royal Decree on 24 April — closes 8 May. **Belgium**'s federal Minister Clarinval reaffirmed the ambition to fully transpose by 7 June, but no draft text yet. **Czechia** closed its consultation on 27 April and is openly opting for a "minimalist" transposition (substantive obligations only from 2028).

🔴 **Ireland** has now confirmed it will miss the deadline; a Mason Hayes & Curran survey of 500 employers found 1 in 5 see the rules as an "unnecessary burden." **Estonia** softened its earlier "we'd rather pay the fine" stance — salary-history bans and pay-range disclosure stay in.
